What’s The Deal With GameStop?

A Quick Glance Into GME


(note that GME has yet to release Q3 and Q4 data in 2023) In 2020, all four quarters saw the lowest revenue compared to other years, with the second quarter (Q2) having the absolute lowest at $942 million. In contrast, the highest revenue was observed in the fourth quarter (Q4) of 2021, totaling to $2,253.9 billion. Between 2020 and 2021, each quarter experienced a significant revenue increase compared to other years, with growth rates of 25.05%, 25.63%, 29.05%, and 6.21% for Q1 through Q4, respectively. In comparison, from 2021 to 2022, the revenue changes for each quarter were more modest: 8% in Q1, 4% in Q2, 9% in Q3, and 1% in Q4.

A company’s revenue is an indicator of its overall health. For many investors, it is a key quantity that plays a part in their decisions relating to a company’s stock. And, for the company, it is an important quantity that can drastically or minimally change how their company should function in order to meet their ultimate goal: make money. If we are just looking at revenue, the years 2020 and 2021 were great years for GME financially. Especially in 2021, with three of its four quarters experiencing more than 25% increases in revenue compared to the quarters in 2020.


In every quarter except Q4, GME experienced a “negative” net income. The largest being in Q1 of 2020 with a net income of -$165.7 million. From 2020 to 2021, beginning at Q1, the net income ratio was 0.4031, 0.5535, 5.6064, and -1.8323 (a negative ratio indicates a change in signs). The largest “positive” net income is observed in Q4 of 2020, amounting to $80.5 million.

In short, net income is a key financial metric that can provide insights into a company’s profitability. A higher net income generally indicates good financial health, making the company more attractive to potential investors and hence a greater potential for company growth. For the most part, GME has experienced a net loss in each quarter. That is, their total expenses almost always exceeds their revenue generated. Does this mean they are performing poorly financially since 2020? Not exactly. GME could have a positive cash flow from its operations which is crucial in meeting day to day operations and planning. However, GME’s net losses will more than likely raise concerns about it’s financial health. Concerns which can be supported by a closer look into GME’s cash flows throughout the years.
